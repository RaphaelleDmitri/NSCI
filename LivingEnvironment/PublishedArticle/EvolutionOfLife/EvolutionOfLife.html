Article Title: Revisiting the State of Philippine Biodiversity" highlights evolutionary aspects of life by emphasizing conservation priorities for unique species and ecosystems shaped by the Philippines' biogeographic history.

As a megadiverse country, the Philippines is recognized for its rich biodiversity. It has the most diverse life forms on a per unit area. Its biodiversity, composed of fiona and fauna, provides resources for basic need for human survival, promotes economic development and offers environmental services. However the unsustainable use and management of the country’s biodiversity may leads to its destruction. The country has been tagged as one of the world’s biodiversity hotspots and top priority in terms of conservation. Policies has been initiated to protect and conserve the biodiversity in the country which cover legislations and benefits sharing.
