The Living Environment

The living environment is the basic knowledge about how living things function and how they interact with one another and their environment. It is represented by the space in which organisms live and interact with each other or with the non-living environment.
The living environment focuses on six major subjects.

o the diversity of life, as reflected in the biological characteristics of the earth's organisms
o the transfer of heritable characteristics from one generation to the next
o the structure and functioning of cells, the basic building blocks of all organisms
o the interdependence of all organisms and their environment
o the flow of matter and energy through the cycles of life
o how biological evolution explains the similarity and diversity of life.

Importance

The living environment is essential for human survival and well-being, providing fundamental services such as clean air, drinking water, food, shelter, biological diversity, and climate regulating services. It focuses on six concepts of ideas: Diversity of Life – which allows ecosystems to be resilient and resources to be available; Heredity – which allows for species to adapt as information is passed on; Flow of Matter and Energy – that allow nutrients and energy to flow through systems to sustain life; Evolution of Life – an explanation for adaptation over time to ever-changing environments; and Interdependence of Life – a reminder that everything requires other things in order to live. In conclusion, the five subjects show the relationship between organisms and their environment. To save various living beings, it is important for people to efficiently use various resources and learn about genes and relations. It will help in the thriving of the biosphere.
