The Physical setting

The Physical setting in science is about how the Universe and the Earth formed and the processes in it. It includes the composition of the universe, how big it is and how it formed, the earth and its composition, as well as the processes that shaped it, the structure of the matter, the energy transformations, the motion, and the forces of nature.

Importance

Physical setting provides context and scientific knowledge about the Universe, the Earth and the processes that shape it. It also affects the behavior of humans and improves both their physical and mental health through the natural beauty of the environment. In addition, Physical setting also provides context about the understanding of natural events, processes and phenomena that influence scientific inquiry and also leave an impact in different disciplines such as in environmental science, ecology and urban planning. It also explains how the world is made and creates a beautiful landscape and seascape through natural occurrences and patterns. Physical Setting doesn't only focus on natural events but also how the humans modified the natural phenomenons and events in the world that somehow altered the natural processes and created a man-made landscape and seascape.
