The Universe

Earth only existed for about one third of the history of the universe. Our universe contains billions of galaxies including the Milky Way Galaxy, which is the galaxy where Earth is located. The Milky Way Galaxy is an ordinary disk-shaped galaxy that consists of billions of stars. Near the edge of the galaxy, there is an orbiting medium-sized star, which is the sun.

Galaxies

Using the fastest rocket, it will take a thousand years to reach the star near the sun, four years for the light of the nearest star to reach the Earth, and it will take not long after the beginning of the universe for the light of the nearest galaxy to reach us.

It came from an explosion of a single dot of a hot, dense, and chaotic mass more than ten billion years ago. The stars are formed from dense clouds of gases made by the lightest elements, which are hydrogen and helium. It will continue to heat up and begin releasing nuclear energy from the fusion of lighter elements to heavier elements in its core. After some time, it will explode which also affects the celestial bodies near it, and it will again create a cloud of gas and continue the cycle of star formation.

stars

Our solar system also came from the clouds of gas from the wake of the exploded stars about 5 billion years ago. Everything in and on Earth is made of these elements. The heavier elements fall at the center of the planets, while the lightest elements were mostly blown or boiled away from the sun (for the planets near the sun, such as Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars), and surround the planets, such as Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, Neptune, and Pluto, as deep atmosphere of gas or frozen solid layer.

There is a star that is smaller or bigger, and cooler and hotter than the sun, but they are part of a system of two or more stars orbiting around a common center of mass. There are also other galaxies that are different in size, shape and the direction of motion. Despite these differences, the elements, forces, and forms of energy are the same to our solar system and galaxy. They also behave according to physical principles.
