Processes that Shape the Earth

The continual changes occurring in the surface of the Earth are caused by the forces within it. As the separate crustal plates move above the hot, almost molten or softer layer, an inch or more per year, some of it sliding past each other, convergent or colliding, and moving away. When the crustal plates converge or collide it creates mountain ranges, when one plate slides on another and sinks it also creates mountains because of the Earthquake that breaks the surface, and volcanic eruptions that release molten rock from below, when it moves away from each other under the continent it will create a valley, and when it occurs in ocean basins it will also widen the ocean floor. When volcanic eruptions happen in the mid-ocean it may create islands and mountains higher than mountains in land.

Plate tectonics

Waves, wind, and ice also play a role in sculpting the Earth's surface. The waves eroded the shoreline by carrying sediments and breaking the rocks, as well as the glacial ice that deposits the sediments that it carries to the sea, and the wind carries off loose surface materials and deposits or transfers it elsewhere.

The elements such as carbon, sulfur, oxygen, and nitrogen change the location and the chemical compositions of land, ocean, and atmosphere as it cycles in and on it. The minerals are made, dissolved, and remade again on the surface and buried beneath the surface. Solid rocks are formed by the shells of dead organisms and sand that is buried and solidified by the dissolved minerals. The sedimentary rock is also buried enough and undergoes the process of melting and crystallization because of the heat and pressure, and it forms other types of rock. As this cycle continues it creates another surface and sometimes mountain.

geological formations

Plants, animals, humans and other organisms also shaped the Earth by changing the soil's composition and texture, altering the atmospheres, and also increasing the emissions of carbon dioxide that increase the temperature of the Earth.
