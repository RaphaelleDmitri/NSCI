The Earth

It is the third planet from the sun that is spherical in shape. It is mostly made of rock, a relatively thin layer of water that covers almost three-fourths of it, and is enveloped by a thin blanket of air.

Earth's structure

It is the only planet that supports life. The gravitational pull of the planet's mass is enough to hold onto an atmosphere that evolved because of the physical conditions on the Earth's surface, the evolution of plant life, and it is an integral part of the global ecosystem.

The distance of Earth and the Sun ensures that the Earth can have enough energy to sustain life and not so fast that it can quickly boil away water and the necessary molecules that may cause life would not form.

The one-year revolution of the Earth around the Sun results in the seasonal variations of climate, the rotation of the Earth on its axis for 24 hours produces the night-and-day cycle, and the combination of Earth's motion and the moon's orbit around the Earth once in 28 days produces the phases of the moon.
