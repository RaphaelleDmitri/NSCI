Structure of Matter

Based on the modern theory of matter, elements consist of different tiny particles called atoms that join together in a different configuration to create new substances.

Each atom is composed of a positive charge (protons) nucleus at the center, and it is surrounded by negatively charged electrons. It also contains a neutral charge called neutron, that is added to its mass but does not affect the number of electrons.

Matter can exist in different states such as solid, liquid, and gas. The state of matter is affected by the temperature and pressure. If the temperature is cooler, atoms or molecules packed together in solid state. If the temperature increases the energy also increases, as well as, the motion of an atom that enables them to slide past each other like in the liquid state. When the temperature increases more the attraction becomes lesser that enables them to move freely, as it is in gas.
