Definition of Science

a naturalist in the rainforest, Einstein’s equations scribbled on a chalkboard, the launch of the space shuttle, bubbling beaker.

The Physical Setting

The Physical setting in science is about how the Universe and the Earth formed and the processes in it. It includes the composition of the universe, how big it is and how it formed, the earth and its composition, as well as the processes that shaped it, the structure of the matter, the energy transformations, the motion, and the forces of nature.

The Living Environment

The living environment is the basic knowledge about how living things function and how they interact with one another and their environment. It is represented by the space in which organisms live and interact with each other or with the non-living environment.
